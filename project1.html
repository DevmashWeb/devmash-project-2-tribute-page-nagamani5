SIR.M.VISHVESHWARAYYA

DIWAN OF MYSORE

Born

:15 September 1860

muddenahalli,chickkaballapur.

Died

:12 April 1962

Bangalore,Mysore State,India.

Nationality

:Indian

Profession

:Civil Engineer


Mokshagundam Vishveshwarayya was born in a telugu family,to Mokshagundam srinivasa shastry and venkatalakshmamma in Muddenahalli village. Vishveshwarayya took a job with the public works depsrtment(PWD) of Mumbai and was later invited to join the indian irrigation commission.He implemented an extremly intricate system of irrigation in the Deccan area.He also designed and patented a system of automatic weir water floodgates that were first installed in 1903 at the Khadakvasla Reservoir near Pune.Based on the success of these gates,the same system was installed at the Tigra Dam in Gwalior and the Krishnaraja Sagara(KRS)Dam in Mandya/Mysore,Karnataka.


He was called the "Father Of Modern Mysore State":During his service with the government of Mysore state,he was responsible for the founding of the Mysore Soap Factory,the Parasitoide Laboratory,the Mysore Iron and Steel Works in Bhadravathi,the Sri Jayachamarajendra Polytechnic Institute,the Bangalore Agricultural University,the State Bank Of Mysore,The Century Club,Mysore Chambers Of Commerce,University Vishveshwarayya College Of Engineering, etc.He was known for sincerity,time management and dedication to a cause.


With the support of Krishnaraja Wodeyar IV,Maharaja of Mysore,Vishveshwarayya made good contribution as Diwan to the all round development of Mysore state.He was awarded with nation's highest honour,the Bharat Ratna,in 1955.Sir,M.V. was awarded honorary Membership of London Institution Of Civil Engineers. He was also awarded a fellowship of the Indian Institute Of Science.He was awarded several honorary doctoral degrees including D.Sc.,LL.D.Litt.from eight universities in India.He was president of the 1923 session of the Indian Science Congress.He was the most popular person from Karnataka,in a newspaper survey conducted by Prajavani.
